Step 03a

5 Winning Formula Templates

Every template below is derived directly from what worked in both datasets. Not theory — patterns from actual high-performing posts.

01
The Incomplete Statement
Hook One sentence, no period, no explanation. Leaves a gap the viewer's brain must resolve. e.g. "Who're you in love if you're not so obsessed…" / "At least one of us happy now…"
Body Slow reveal of the emotion or story behind the statement. 30-45 seconds. Voiceover or text on screen. No explanation in the first 5 seconds — earn it.
CTA Ask a direct binary question. "Have you felt this?" or "Send this to someone who needs to hear it." Never "follow for more."
02
The Reframe Hook
Hook Challenges a belief the audience holds. Uses "not just" or "everyone talks about X but nobody talks about Y." e.g. "Letting go isn't just about losing them — you lose a version of yourself too." / "They just needed you, not advice."
Body 3-4 specific, concrete observations that prove the reframe. Not vague. "The habits you formed. The inside jokes that have nowhere to go. The version of you that only existed with them."
CTA Debate prompt. "Agree or disagree?" / "Am I wrong?" High comment trigger.
03
The Group Call-Out
Hook Name a specific type of person in the first 2 seconds. e.g. "This is for people who love too deeply." / "If you've ever been the one who cares more — this is for you." / "People who overthink at 3am, hear me out."
Body Validate what that group feels. Use second person ("you") throughout. Make it feel like you're describing them, not at them. 20-35 seconds.
CTA "Tag someone who needs this." / "Send this to someone who gets it." Forces shares — the highest-value engagement signal for reach.
04
The Bold Declarative
Hook A single sentence that is an opinion stated as fact. Short, confident, no hedging. e.g. "Choose your people wisely." / "Just STAY." / "Ego kills everything." — works best with 1-3 words or one clean clause.
Body Emotional evidence for the statement. Story-driven or poetic. Music-heavy. Under 30 seconds. Let the visuals and audio do the heavy lifting here — minimal text.
CTA No question. Just: "Send to someone who needs to hear this." The declarative nature already creates the share impulse.
05
The Series / Streak Format
Hook Open with the series number. "Day 1/21 — [bold emotional statement]" or "21 days of unlearning: Day 1." The number creates commitment device psychology — viewers want to see where it goes.
Body Each episode covers one specific emotion, lesson, or story. Consistent format, consistent music, consistent visual style. The reliability IS the content.
CTA "Follow so you don't miss Day 2." Only time a follow CTA is justified — because there's a reason to follow, not just a generic ask.
